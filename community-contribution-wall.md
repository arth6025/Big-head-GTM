# Community Contribution Wall — Product & Community Concept
**Big Head Insights Wall: Where Shopify AI Visibility Practitioners Share What's Actually Working**

---

## What It Is

The Big Head Insights Wall is a community-powered knowledge resource where Shopify SEO, AEO (Answer Engine Optimization), and GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) practitioners contribute real results from real stores.

Think Product Hunt meets a curated case study database — but specifically for people doing AI visibility work on Shopify. Not theory. Not thought leadership fluff. Actual prompt wins, schema tactics, content formats, and before/after audit scores from people doing the work.

Anyone can browse. Verified practitioners submit. The best contributions get featured, shared, and linked back to audits so others can test the same tactic on their own store.

This is a living, practitioner-maintained reference for a discipline — GEO for Shopify — that has no authoritative home yet.

## Why Big Head Should Own This

Big Head has a natural claim on this territory that no one else has staked. Reasons:

**1. The data gives it credibility no blog can fake.**
Every tip submitted to the Insights Wall can link to a real Big Head audit showing the before and after. That verification layer is unique. SEMrush can publish GEO content. Ahrefs can. But only Big Head can show a practitioner's claim validated against live citation data across four AI engines.

**2. It builds an audience that extends beyond product users.**
Tool audiences plateau when acquisition slows. Community audiences compound. A practitioner who contributes to the Insights Wall is not necessarily a Big Head customer — but they're in the ecosystem, they're driving traffic to audit CTAs, and they're telling their clients about Big Head. The community becomes a distribution channel.

**3. It establishes Big Head as the category owner for Shopify GEO.**
Right now, there is no single destination for Shopify-specific AI visibility knowledge. The field is young enough that whoever builds the definitive resource first owns the category association. If Big Head builds this wall and maintains it, "Shopify GEO" becomes synonymous with Big Head in the same way that "link building" became synonymous with Ahrefs.

**4. It creates SEO and GEO value simultaneously.**
A well-structured Insights Wall generates long-tail search traffic (practitioners Googling specific tactics) and gets cited in AI engines (LLMs referencing the resource as a knowledge base). The meta-win is real: Big Head gets cited by AI engines for being the authoritative source on AI citation optimization.

## What Contributors Submit

Four contribution types, each with a defined template:

**1. Prompt Wins**
A specific prompt query that started generating citations after a content change or optimization. Format: the query, which AI engine(s) cited the store, what content change drove the citation, how many citations in the first week. Example: "We added a 200-word FAQ section on protein absorption and this prompt query on Perplexity started citing us 47 times in the first 7 days."

**2. Content Formats That Get Cited**
Structural observations: FAQs get cited more than product descriptions. Comparison tables outperform long-form guides for product queries on ChatGPT. Ingredient glossaries work for supplements. Numbered lists get pulled into Perplexity snippets. Format: observation, category it applies to, estimated lift, link to example page (optional).

**3. Technical Tips**
Schema markup implementations, structured data formats, meta field configurations, internal linking patterns that affected citation visibility. Format: the change, how to implement it on Shopify, before/after audit data if available.

**4. Case Studies**
The highest-value contribution type. Full before/after audit comparison with methodology. What the store was doing, what changed, what the score did over 30 or 60 days. Can be anonymous (brand name withheld, category disclosed). Big Head team verifies against audit data before featuring.

## Curation Model

**Who moderates:** A small Big Head editorial layer (initially the founding team; eventually a community moderator role) reviews every submission before it goes live. This is not fully open UGC. Quality control is the wall's core value proposition.

**Review criteria:**
- Is the claim specific and verifiable? (Not "we updated our content and got more citations" — that's not a tip)
- Does the contributor have a Big Head audit to back it up, or is the claim plausible and reproducible?
- Is it Shopify-specific or is it generic SEO advice dressed up in GEO language?

**What gets featured (top of wall / social distribution):**
- Case studies with verified audit data showing >20% visibility score improvement
- Prompt wins with unusually high citation counts
- Technical tips that can be implemented in under 30 minutes
- Contributions from practitioners with significant audiences who are likely to amplify the feature

**Quality maintenance over time:**
- Contributions older than 90 days are flagged for "still relevant?" review — AI engine behavior changes fast
- Contributors can update their submissions rather than re-submitting
- Downvoting from verified practitioners (not public) allows community self-moderation without mob dynamics

## How It Drives PLG

Every surface on the Insights Wall has a single job: move readers toward running an audit.

**The verify CTA:**
Every contribution — every single one — ends with: "Try this on your own store → [Run a free Big Head audit]". The link is contextual: if the tip is about FAQ schema, the audit CTA pre-populates the store's FAQ pages. Low friction, high relevance.

**Contributor profiles and referral links:**
Contributors get a public profile page on the Insights Wall showing all their contributions, their verified store's audit summary (if they consent), and a unique referral link. Every referral that converts to a paid plan earns the contributor something — platform credit, early access to features, or a public "Verified Expert" badge. This turns contributors into a micro-affiliate network without the complexity of a formal affiliate program.

**Featured contributions → social → sign-ups:**
When a contribution gets featured on the Insights Wall, Big Head publishes it on social (Twitter/X, LinkedIn) with attribution to the contributor. The contributor shares. Their audience sees Big Head for the first time. Some percentage clicks through to the wall, reads more, runs an audit. This flywheel requires no ad spend.

**Leaderboard integration:**
The Insights Wall can pull a "Top Contributors This Month" module into the main leaderboard product. This cross-pollinates the two properties and gives contributors a reason to submit consistently.

## Who to Seed It With First

Do not open the wall publicly before you have at least 30 high-quality contributions. A wall with 4 entries is worse than no wall. Seed in two phases:

**Phase 1 — Closed Beta (8–12 people, 4 weeks before public launch):**

- 3–4 Shopify Plus agency founders or senior SEO strategists with strong Twitter/LinkedIn followings in DTC
- 2–3 prominent DTC growth accounts on Twitter (the type who run "what's working in growth" threads)
- 1–2 technical Shopify experts (developers known for structured data or headless Shopify work)
- Klaviyo or Postscript ecosystem partners who work closely with DTC brands on content — they understand the AI email/content overlap and have client proof points
- 1–2 active contributors to r/Entrepreneur DTC discussions (check for people who write detailed, specific posts — they will write detailed, specific wall contributions)

Ask them privately: "We're building a knowledge wall for Shopify AI visibility practitioners. We want you to be one of the first 10 contributors. Here's what it is. Would you submit one thing you've seen work?" Keep it personal and low-pressure.

**Phase 2 — Semi-public (invite-only, 2 weeks before launch):**
Open submissions to Big Head's existing paid user base. These are people who have already run audits, have real data, and have a vested interest in the category maturing. They are your best early contributors.

**Launch day:** Open public submissions with the seed content already in place.

## Build vs. Use Existing Platform

Four real options, assessed honestly:

**Option A: Build custom (integrated into shopos.ai/agents/big-head)**
Best long-term. The wall lives inside the Big Head product, audit CTAs are deeply integrated, contributor profiles link directly to audit data, and the wall feeds into the product's data layer. High development cost. Minimum 6–8 weeks to build properly.

**Option B: Notion + Zapier (fastest to launch)**
A structured Notion database with a Tally or Typeform intake form, Zapier routing submissions to a moderation queue, and a public Notion page as the wall. Zero dev cost. Functional in a week. Ugly, not scalable, no referral tracking. Good for validating the concept before committing dev resources.

**Option C: Circle.so**
Purpose-built community platform with discussion threads, member profiles, and resource libraries. Can be stood up in a week, looks professional, supports upvoting and curation. Cost: ~$99–$399/month. The wall becomes a section within a broader Big Head community space. Downside: it's behind a login, which reduces SEO value and open browsability.

**Option D: Beehiiv as a newsletter layer over a custom wall**
The wall is custom-built (or Notion-based initially), but the best contributions each week are compiled into a "Big Head Insights" newsletter delivered via Beehiiv. The newsletter becomes its own distribution channel with a subscriber list that Big Head owns. This is the highest long-term leverage option if the newsletter gains traction.

**Recommendation:** Start with Notion + Zapier to validate (2 weeks), then build custom once you have 50+ contributions and evidence of organic sharing behavior. Use Beehiiv as the newsletter layer from day one — it's low cost and starts building a subscriber list immediately.
