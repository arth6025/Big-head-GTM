## Campaign Concept

**"The AI Search Rankings for Shopify Brands — Revealed."**

Big Head publishes the first-ever public ranking of the top 100 Shopify brands by AI Visibility Score: which brands are being cited by ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and Claude — and which are invisible.

This is a data release, not a blog post. It functions like a Glassdoor score for DTC brands: everyone wants to see where they rank, nobody wants to be at the bottom, and agencies will print it and take it to client meetings.

---

## Why This Lands

Three audiences, three different motivations:

**Ranked brands** will share it. A top-20 ranking is a badge. Founders and CMOs will post it on LinkedIn and Twitter because it validates their content strategy in a way that traditional SEO rankings never could. The social proof is novel: "we're winning AI search" is a new flex that hasn't been staked yet.

**Unranked brands** will sign up. Any brand that searches for their name and doesn't see it in the top 100 has an immediate question: where am I? Big Head is the only place to answer it. The leaderboard is a mirror that shows hundreds of brands their own gap.

**Agencies** will use it as a client education tool. A Shopify agency can walk into a pitch with this leaderboard and say: "Your competitor is ranked 14th for AI visibility. You're not on the list." That conversation sells the agency's services and sends the brand to Big Head. This is a distribution multiplier that requires zero agency partnership budget — the data does the selling.

## The Data

The leaderboard shows, for each of the top 100 Shopify brands:

- Overall AI Visibility Score (Big Head's proprietary composite)
- Rank in their category (e.g., #3 in Footwear, #1 in Supplements)
- Citation volume across each of the 4 AI engines: ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Claude
- Top cited prompt category (e.g., "product comparisons," "ingredient education," "gifting")
- Week-over-week score movement (rising or falling)

**What the PMM needs to coordinate with data and engineering:**

This requires Big Head to run full audits on the top 100 Shopify brands globally — not just user-submitted stores. Specifically:

1. **Brand list definition** — PMM needs to work with data team to define "top 100" (by estimated GMV, Shopify merchant tier, social following, or a combination). This is an editorial decision that affects credibility. Suggestion: use a combination of Shopify Plus merchant list, SimilarWeb traffic ranking, and social follower count to derive a defensible methodology.
2. **Audit infrastructure at scale** — Engineering needs to confirm Big Head's audit pipeline can run 100 brand audits in a controlled window without rate-limiting issues or data quality degradation. Coordinate a dedicated audit run at least 2 weeks before planned release date.
3. **Data freshness window** — All 100 audits should be run within the same 7-day window so scores are comparable. Set a hard cutoff date and lock data. Do not re-run audits after the cutoff or scores will be inconsistent.
4. **Competitive sensitivity** — Some brand data may surface uncomfortable comparisons. Agree internally on how to handle brands that request removal, and draft a brief policy statement before publishing.
5. **Methodology disclosure** — The leaderboard is more credible if it includes a "how we calculated this" section. Data team should prepare a one-page methodology note. Transparency builds trust; opacity invites criticism.

## Visual Format

The goal is a graphic that is immediately readable and irresistibly shareable.

**Primary format: 1:1 (square) for Twitter/X and Instagram**
- Dark background (positions Big Head as a premium data product, not a cheerful startup)
- Top 20 brands shown with rank, name, logo, and score — ranked list style
- Category filter chips across the top (All / Beauty / Apparel / Supplements / Home / Footwear / etc.)
- Big Head logo in the corner, URL bottom-right
- Score bar or dot indicator per brand showing citation distribution across the 4 AI engines

**Secondary format: 16:9 (landscape) for LinkedIn and press**
- Same data, more breathing room
- Space for a subtitle: "Based on [X,XXX] prompt queries across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini & Claude — [Month] 2026"

**Interactive web version:**
- Full top 100 with filter by category, AI engine, score range
- Each brand row expandable to show their top cited queries
- CTA on every row: "Check your store's score →" linking to the Big Head audit
- This is the version that drives actual sign-ups; the social graphic drives awareness

Design directive: the visual should look like it belongs in a TechCrunch article or a VC deck, not a product update. Think Andreessen Horowitz data charts or Morning Consult brand trackers.

## Seeding Strategy

Reach out to top-ranked brands BEFORE publishing. Specifically:

1. **Identify the top 10 brands** in the final leaderboard at least 5 business days before release.
2. **Email their marketing/growth leads** directly. Message: "Your brand ranked #X on Big Head's first AI Visibility Leaderboard for Shopify brands. We're publishing it on [date]. Here's a preview of your entry and a shareable graphic we made for you. Would love for you to share it the day it drops."
3. **Prepare a custom shareable asset** for the top 10 — a 1:1 graphic with just their logo, rank, and score. Make sharing frictionless.
4. **Offer a short interview** to the #1 ranked brand for a companion post ("How [Brand] Became the Most AI-Visible Shopify Brand in [Category]"). This becomes its own content moment and deepens their investment in the story.
5. **Do not ask for anything in return.** No formal partnership, no affiliate deal. The only ask is that they share if they like it. Keep the relationship clean — it pays off later.

## Timing

**Best release window:** Tuesday or Wednesday, 9am ET.

Avoid:
- Mondays (competition with inbox clearing; lower engagement)
- Fridays (news cycle dead zone for DTC)
- Any week with a major Shopify event or conference (your story will get buried)
- Any week a major DTC brand is in the press for a negative reason (adjacency risk)

**Recommended release month:** First week of a new quarter (October, January, April) tends to drive planning-mode engagement. Brand and agency teams are setting priorities and this lands as a strategic input, not just a data point.

If the data is ready in May/June 2026, consider holding for early Q3 (first week of July) when the DTC community is in mid-year planning mode and the news cycle is lighter.
